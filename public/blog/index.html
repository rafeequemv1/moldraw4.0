What is MolDraw?

A Free ChemDraw Alternative for Molecular Structure Editing and 3D Visualization

MolDraw is a powerful, web-based molecular structure editor that serves as a completely free alternative to ChemDraw. Built by Scidart Academy, MolDraw empowers chemists, researchers, students, and educators to draw, edit, visualize, and export molecular structures without any cost or subscription fees.

Why Choose MolDraw?

Traditional molecular drawing software like ChemDraw often comes with expensive licenses that can be prohibitive for students, independent researchers, or small labs. MolDraw breaks down these barriers by offering professional-grade molecular editing capabilities entirely free of charge, accessible directly through your web browser.

üé® Intuitive 2D Structure Editor

MolDraw features a sophisticated 2D molecular structure editor powered by Ketcher, an open-source chemical structure editor. Draw complex organic molecules, add functional groups, create rings, and modify bonds with ease. The interface is clean, responsive, and designed for both beginners and experienced chemists.

üî¨ Real-Time 3D Visualization

One of MolDraw's standout features is its integrated 3D molecular viewer. As you draw or modify a structure in 2D, you can instantly see it rendered in three dimensions. Switch between different visualization styles including:

  • Ball & Stick: Classic representation showing atoms as spheres and bonds as sticks
  • Stick: Simplified view focusing on the molecular framework
  • Space-Fill: Van der Waals radii representation showing molecular volume
  • Line: Minimal wireframe view for clarity

üîç Molecule Search & Identification

Need to work with a known compound? MolDraw integrates with PubChem to search molecules by name. Simply type a molecule name (like "aspirin", "caffeine", or "benzene") and MolDraw will fetch the structure and load it into the editor. The system can also identify molecules you've drawn and suggest their IUPAC names.

üì§ Multiple Export Formats

Export your molecular structures in various formats to suit your needs:

  • PNG: High-quality images with transparent backgrounds for presentations and publications
  • SDF: Structure Data Format for compatibility with other chemistry software
  • XYZ: Coordinate format for computational chemistry
  • X3D: 3D model format with bonds for web visualization
  • OBJ: 3D model format compatible with Blender and other 3D software

Who Can Use MolDraw?

MolDraw is designed for anyone who works with molecular structures:

  • Students: Perfect for organic chemistry courses, lab reports, and homework assignments
  • Researchers: Quick structure drawing for papers, presentations, and research documentation
  • Educators: Create teaching materials, exam questions, and visual aids without software costs
  • Pharmaceutical Scientists: Draw drug molecules and analyze their structures
  • Anyone: Free access means no barriers to entry for hobbyists or curious learners

Built on Open Source

MolDraw is built using Ketcher, an open-source chemical structure editor developed by EPAM Systems. This ensures reliability, transparency, and continuous improvement through community contributions. The 3D visualization is powered by 3Dmol.js, a powerful molecular visualization library.

Getting Started

Using MolDraw is simple:

  1. Visit the MolDraw website
  2. Start drawing in the left panel using the intuitive tools
  3. Watch your molecule appear in 3D on the right
  4. Adjust visualization settings, toggle hydrogens, or change rendering styles
  5. Export your structure in your preferred format

No account creation, no downloads, no credit card required. Just open your browser and start creating.
